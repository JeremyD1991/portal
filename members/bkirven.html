Butch Kirven

President of the Kirven Company

Elected in 2004, H. G “Butch” Kirven is currently in his fourth term as a member of Greenville County Council, serving as its chairman from 2005-2013 and from 2017-present. He also serves as the chairman of GPATS, the regional metropolitan planning organization (MPO) for the urbanized areas of Greenville and Pickens counties including a small portion of Anderson County.

Butch served in the Army National Guard for over 35-years, retiring in July 2003. As a brigadier general, he was one of the first general officers called to active duty in the US Army following the 9/11 attacks on the United States. He initially served as a special advisor to the FORSCOM commander on air and missile defense readiness and also served as the deputy commanding general of the 263rd Army Air and Missile Defense Command (Operation Noble Eagle).

Butch and his wife, Diane, live near Simpsonville, in southeastern Greenville County, where he maintains an office as a real estate appraiser and consultant. He is a graduate of Presbyterian College and the US Army War College.
